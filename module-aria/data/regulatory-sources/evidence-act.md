# Foundations for Evidence-Based Policymaking Act (Evidence Act)

**Source ID:** `evidence-act`
**Authority:** Public Law 115-435
**Scope (CLEAR):** Evidence basis for findings, analyses, and reported conclusions.

> SYNTHETIC SUMMARY — not a legally complete restatement of the Evidence Act.
> These are the specific, deterministic rules the CLEAR engine evaluates an output
> against. A working subset for demonstration on synthetic data only.

## Rules CLEAR evaluates

1. **R-EV-1 — Evidence basis present.** Every analytical finding or reported
   conclusion must cite an evidence basis (source data, evaluation, or measured
   result). A conclusion asserted without an evidence basis is non-compliant.

2. **R-EV-2 — Data quality sufficient for evidence.** Evidence drawn from pipeline
   materials must meet the platform data-quality threshold (90%). Evidence below
   threshold is flagged at risk (amber); below threshold on a congressional
   submission is a priority (P1) violation.

## Notes

The Evidence Act rule reinforces that CLEAR does not assert compliance — it checks
whether the output carries the evidentiary basis the Act requires, and a human
reviewer certifies the result.
