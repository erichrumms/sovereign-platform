# SOVEREIGN Platform — CPMI Independent Validation Architecture
**External Check Above CPMI's Certification Function**

Version: 1.0 — May 2026  
Authority: Project Principal · SOVEREIGN Platform Governance Authority  
Status: APPROVED — incorporated into Integration Brief v1.3

---

## 1. The Problem This Document Resolves

CPMI is simultaneously the certifying authority for all six SOVEREIGN products and itself subject to the same certification it issues. The panel correctly identified that this creates a governance risk: a system that is the final word on its own outputs is a single point of failure with no check above it. Documentation of reasoning is not the same as external validation of that reasoning.

This document defines the independent validation layer that sits above CPMI's certification function — what it is, where it lives in the Option C shell architecture, what it requires, and what it cannot do.

---

## 2. The Distinction That Must Be Maintained

The panel drew a critical distinction that SOVEREIGN must preserve: the difference between a genuine derivation that a reviewer can follow and challenge, and a post-hoc rationalization generated by the same model that produced the recommendation.

CPMI's six-step reasoning chain is designed to be a genuine derivation — each step constrained by the prior step's output, with drift detection if confidence drops more than 15% from session baseline. The chain is logged event-by-event, not reconstructed after the recommendation.

However, the panel is correct that logging the chain is not the same as externally validating the chain. The Logger confirms what CPMI produced. It does not confirm that what CPMI produced is correct. That is the function of the independent validation layer.

---

## 3. The Independent Validation Layer — Architecture

The independent validation layer has three components. They operate at different levels and serve different purposes.

### Component 1 — The Human Independent Reviewer (Gate 3 — Existing, Strengthened)

CPMI-VRS Gate 3 already requires a human independent reviewer to attest that they have reviewed the reasoning chain and the recommendation before high-consequence outputs are acted upon. This is the primary check above CPMI's certification function.

**What "independent" means in Gate 3:** The reviewer must not be the same person who initiated the governance query, and must not be the same person who will act on the recommendation. This is enforced by the decision matrix (no self-approval) and logged with identity.

**What the Gate 3 reviewer is checking:** Not whether CPMI's reasoning is technically correct in every detail — the reviewer is a domain expert, not a model auditor. The reviewer is checking whether:
- The recommendation is plausible given what they know about the program
- The stated assumptions in Step 4 are reasonable
- The confidence score in Step 5 is consistent with the reviewer's own assessment of the situation
- Any stated drift flag (REASONING_DRIFT event) has been addressed

**What Gate 3 cannot do:** A human reviewer cannot catch subtle errors in CPMI's rule application if the reviewer is not also expert in the specific regulatory norm being applied. Gate 3 is a judgment check, not a compliance audit. This limitation is real and must be stated.

### Component 2 — The Behavioral Benchmark Suite (Existing, Required)

The CPMI behavioral benchmarks defined in the Prompt Registry Specification (five benchmarks covering six-step completeness, uncertainty surfacing, Gate 3 escalation trigger, drift detection, and constraint adherence) constitute an ongoing external check on CPMI's behavior.

**These benchmarks are not run once — they are run before every prompt change and on a defined periodic schedule.** The periodic schedule is: monthly during active development, quarterly in production.

**What the benchmarks catch:** Prompt drift, model version changes that alter reasoning behavior, configuration errors that cause CPMI to skip steps or suppress uncertainty. The benchmarks do not catch factual errors in CPMI's world model knowledge — they catch behavioral deviations from the specified reasoning chain.

**New benchmark required — Norm Accuracy Benchmark:** A benchmark that tests CPMI's application of specific regulatory norms against ground-truth expert determinations. For each norm in the active world model, at least two test cases must exist: one where CPMI should apply the norm and one where it should not. Human expert review confirms the expected output. This benchmark does not exist yet — it is a Stage 3 deliverable.

### Component 3 — The Shell-Level Governance Dashboard (Option C Architecture)

In the Option C monorepo, the sovereign-shell hosts the CPMI-VRS status dashboard. This dashboard is the platform-level visibility layer above CPMI's certification function. It surfaces:

- Current VRS gate status for all six products
- REASONING_DRIFT events — flagged immediately, not buried in logs
- Confidence score trends across sessions (declining confidence may indicate world model staleness or norm conflicts)
- Gate 3 attestation queue — pending independent reviews
- Certification expiry tracking

**The dashboard is not CPMI's output — it is a view of CPMI's behavior that the platform administrator and Project Principal see independently of any individual governance query.** It provides the external vantage point that single-product review cannot provide.

## 4. The Self-Certification Problem — Directly Addressed

The panel's concern: a system that certifies its own outputs has no external check. SOVEREIGN's architectural response has three layers:

**Layer 1 (runtime):** Every high-consequence CPMI output requires a Gate 3 human attestation before it is acted upon. The human is the external check at the point of use.

**Layer 2 (behavioral):** The benchmark suite constitutes a periodic external check on CPMI's reasoning behavior that does not depend on CPMI's self-assessment. A benchmark failure stops prompt deployment regardless of what CPMI says about its own output.

**Layer 3 (platform):** The shell-level governance dashboard provides platform-administrator visibility into CPMI's behavior over time. Anomalies visible at the portfolio level — consistent drift in a particular direction, declining confidence trends, unusual Gate 3 escalation patterns — are detectable by the platform administrator without going through CPMI's certification function.

**What this does not resolve:** If the regulatory norms in CPMI's world model are incorrect, all three layers will operate on a flawed foundation. An incorrect norm that CPMI applies consistently will produce consistent Gate 3 attestations from human reviewers who are not themselves expert in that specific norm. This is the residual risk of any governance system that depends on the quality of its knowledge base. The mitigation is the Norm Accuracy Benchmark (Component 2 above) and the Rule Maintenance Process for CPMI's world model.

---

## 5. What "CPMI Certifies Itself" Does and Does Not Mean

The accurate statement: CPMI issues CPMI-VRS certificates. CPMI-VRS Gate 4 (portfolio certification) requires Project Principal approval — not CPMI's own determination that certification is warranted. Gate 3 requires an independent human reviewer. Gate 2 requires Logger chain completeness that is verifiable without CPMI's participation.

The inaccurate reading that the panel correctly flagged: CPMI produces a recommendation, then certifies that the recommendation meets the required standard, with no external check between production and certification. This reading would be correct if Gate 3 were a formality. Gate 3 is architecturally required to be substantive. The Project Principal's Gate 4 approval is the final certification act — CPMI does not certify itself at Gate 4.

The honest limitation: Gate 3's substantiveness depends on the reviewer's domain expertise. The Norm Accuracy Benchmark is the mechanism for verifying that CPMI's reasoning on specific norms is correct. Until that benchmark exists (Stage 3 deliverable), there is a gap in the external validation layer for norm-specific accuracy.
